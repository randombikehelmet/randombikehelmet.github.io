Our Mission

At Whiskaway, we believe every cat deserves a safe and loving home. By fostering a cat, you provide a temporary haven for a cat in need while helping them find their forever family. Fostering is a rewarding experience that saves lives, offers companionship, and makes a lasting difference in your community.

Whether you’re a first-time foster or an experienced caregiver, we’ll guide you through every step of the process. Explore our resources, meet our cats, and discover how you can be part of this life-changing journey.

Ready to open your heart and home? Start fostering today!

Best cat care tips

1. Provide a Balanced Diet: Feed your cat high-quality cat food that meets their nutritional needs. Include both wet and dry food options and ensure fresh water is always available.


2. Regular Vet Checkups: Schedule routine vet visits to monitor your cat’s health, keep up with vaccinations, and prevent any potential health issues.


3. Grooming: Brush your cat regularly to reduce shedding and hairballs. For long-haired breeds, more frequent grooming is needed.


4. Litter Box Maintenance: Clean the litter box daily and provide one box per cat, plus one extra. Keep it in a quiet, accessible place.


5. Enrichment and Play: Provide toys, scratching posts, and interactive play sessions to keep your cat mentally stimulated and physically active.


6. Create a Safe Environment: Make your home cat-proof by removing harmful plants, chemicals, and choking hazards. Ensure your cat has safe places to climb and explore.


7. Provide Fresh Water: Cats often prefer fresh, moving water. Consider using a water fountain to encourage hydration.


8. Spay or Neuter: Spaying or neutering your cat helps prevent overpopulation and can also improve behavior and reduce health risks.


9. Regular Flea and Parasite Prevention: Use vet-recommended flea, tick, and parasite preventatives to protect your cat from external and internal pests.


10. Provide Love and Attention: Cats need affection and companionship. Spend quality time with your cat to build trust and a strong bond, while respecting their independence.
